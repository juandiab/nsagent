**Last updated: June 2, 2026**

The goal of this AUP is simple: use JPilot only on systems you are authorized to manage, and don't use it to harm others.

## 1. Authorized systems only

You may use JPilot only to access, configure, or operate **NetScaler / Citrix ADC appliances and other systems that you own or are expressly authorized to administer.** Connecting to or attempting to act on systems without authorization is strictly prohibited.

JPilot is an **assistive tool** and does **not replace System Administrators or other qualified professionals** — it is meant to optimize their work. Deploy and use it only in **controlled environments with controlled, least-privilege user access**, and keep the host operating system and environment secured. You are responsible for the security of the systems and host on which you run or connect the Service.

By default, the Service enables only the **official vendor documentation sites.** Any additional URL or source you add is enabled **at your own risk**, including the risk of prompt-injection or similar attacks delivered through retrieved web content, appliance output, configuration files, screenshots, or other content the Service ingests.

## 3. AI use

JPilot uses AI that **may produce inaccurate or incomplete output.** You must:

- review and verify AI-suggested commands, configurations, and recommendations before executing them, especially changes affecting production, security, or availability. Confirmation prompts (for example, before potentially destructive commands) are an **assistive safety feature, not a guarantee**, and may not appear for every risky action — you remain responsible for any command you allow to execute;
- not rely on the Service for legal, financial, medical, or other professional advice; and
- not use the Service to generate unlawful, harmful, deceptive, or abusive content, or to attempt to circumvent the safety controls of any connected AI provider; and
- **take responsibility for the data you expose to public/consumer SaaS LLMs.** The platform does not share or expose your confidential information on its own—data leaves only via the prompts and attachments you submit to the provider you configured. Do not submit confidential, regulated, or personal data to a public LLM unless permitted; for sensitive workloads, prefer an Enterprise SaaS, dedicated cloud, or self-hosted LLM.
